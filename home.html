Background

Change

1. Education level changes over time. Talk about how education became more accessible and the effect of having a more educated population.

How Education is Becoming More Accessible:

• Increase in expenditure on education, resulting in more learning institutes and learning material being provided

• Improve compensation for education professionals

• Increase accessibility for low income communities

• The advancement of technology has provided more access to information

• Increased public transportation providing more ways to attend school

Investment

2. The economy has a large effect on education standards. Stronger funding within the education space can greatly increase the quality of education.

Benefits of Investing in Education:

• Helps individuals make better decisions and increases chances in succeeding in life

• On average, each additional year of education boosts a person’s income by 10% and increases a country’s GDP by 18%

• Live healthier lives

• Helps those be more understanding of other people’s needs, as well as society, needs

• Every year across the world 246 million children experience physical violence such as domestic abuse, discrimination, and bullying. Education equips people, young men especially, with the knowledge, attitude, and behaviors required for empathy and critical thinking.

Accessibility

3. Accessibility to education is a key factor in the success of a country's education system.

Increasing Education Accessiblity:

• Reduce cost of education: either reallocate funds from taxes towards buildings and maintain public schools or abolish school fees

• Lunch School Programs: alleviates hunger and encourages attendance

• New Education model: different situations require different methods of teaching: no more dependent on test scores and achievements

• Improve resources for the teachers, have higher-grade students tutor those in the lower-grade

• Providing public transportation to the schools

• Example: Canada is supporting Burkina Faso’s 10-year BRIGHT program by focusing on establishing strong education systems, which enable children and youths, particularly girls, to get a full 10-year cycle of quality basic education. They support activities, such as: building the capacity of government education officials, improving distribution of learning material throughout the country of Burkina Faso, and providing scholarships
